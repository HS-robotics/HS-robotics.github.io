Swerve Driving AMR

Project Overview

  • Developed a compact autonomous mobile robot (AMR) platform utilizing swerve driving mechanism
  • Achieved omnidirectional movement for efficient docking and navigation in cluttered environments
  • Presented at 2019 ROSCon (Macau) Lightning Talk
  • Awarded Grand Prize by the Ministry of Science and ICT at the 2020 Hanium ICT Competition
  • Prize: KRW 5,000,000 (USD 3500) and an iPad

Problem Definition

Conventional mobile robots often face challenges in precise positioning and adaptability in constrained environments. These limitations hinder effective autonomous operation, particularly during docking or executing tasks requiring high maneuverability.

Solution

  • Designed and fabricated a compact swerve drive module with independent steering and driving capabilities
  • Integrated a custom control system using ROS for real-time communication and navigation
  • Developed a joystick-based interface for remote control and feedback mechanisms
  • Conducted modeling and simulation in Gazebo to validate navigation and docking efficiency

Results

  • Achieved precise navigation and omnidirectional movement with minimal space constraints
  • Validated efficiency improvements in docking compared to differential drive robots
  • Demonstrated successful SLAM and Navigation performance in both simulation and physical environments
  • Implemented robust speed and position control using PD and PI controllers

My Contribution

  • Proposed and designed the concept for the swerve driving mechanism
  • Performed detailed CAD modeling and fabrication of the swerve drive module
  • Implemented PID control for steering and driving mechanisms
  • Developed ROS-based simulation environment and conducted SLAM testing
  • Prepared technical documentation for competition and international forums
