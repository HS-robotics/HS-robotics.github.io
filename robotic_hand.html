Project Overview

  • Developed a dexterous robotic hand based on the diaphragm flexure continuum mechanism
  • Enabled unique motion configurations such as finger flexion, 2-DOF thumb motion, finger opposition, and finger crossing
  • Finger cross-motion, a distinctive feature absent in other robotic hands, expands potential applications like sign language
  • Designed with anthropomorphic features, achieving a human-hand size with a total of 10 degrees of freedom, 2-DOF per finger
  • Awarded the Best Paper Award in 2020 (RED Show in 'The 15th Korea Robotics Society Annual Conference'- Undergraduate)

Introduction and Problem Definition

  • Replicating the human hand's adaptability and dexterity is a persistent challenge in robotics
  • Most robotic hands fail to achieve the dexterity and flexibility required for natural human-like motions
  • Current designs lack mechanisms for unique motions such as finger crossing, limiting application versatility

Solution

  • Designed and implemented a diaphragm flexure-based continuum mechanism for enhanced motion flexibility

Results

  • Achieved advanced motion configurations, including precise finger cross movements, using compliant design principles
  • Demonstrated unique motions, including finger crossing, during an academic design competition
  • Recognized as a significant advancement in robotic dexterity through awards and presentations

My Contribution

  • Developed and fabricated the diaphragm flexure-based continuum mechanism and robotic hand
  • Designed and programmed motion configurations for finger flexion, opposition, and cross motion
